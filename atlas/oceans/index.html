Oceans

Oceans absorb ~25% of CO₂; overfishing and plastic are top stressors.

← The Atlas

The picture

The ocean produces much of the oxygen we breathe, absorbs about a quarter of human CO₂ emissions and around 90% of the extra heat we trap. Protecting it - the goal of “30x30” - protects everything in it.

The science

The ocean covers ~71% of Earth’s surface and holds ~97% of its water.

Small actions that reach them

Refuse one single-use plastic
8M+ tonnes of plastic enter the ocean yearly, harming turtles, seabirds and whales.
~2 min
Pick up 10 pieces of trash
Coastal litter is ingested by marine life; small cleanups add up across communities.
~15 min
Choose sustainable seafood
Overfishing and bycatch threaten sharks, turtles and fish stocks.
~10 min
Switch to microplastic-free products
Microbeads and synthetic glitter wash into waterways and enter the food chain.
~10 min
Wash clothes cold and full
Cold, full loads cut energy and shed fewer microplastics into rivers and seas.
~2 min
Switch to reef-safe sunscreen
Common sunscreen ingredients like oxybenzone and octinoxate wash off swimmers and contribute to coral bleaching in popular bays.
~5 min
