Cats

TNR humanely stabilizes community cat populations; indoor cats protect birds.

The picture

Community (feral) cats are best managed humanely with trap-neuter-return, which stabilises colonies over time. Keeping pet cats indoors or supervised protects both the cats and local birds.

The science

Felis catus. A single unspayed pair and their offspring can theoretically produce a large number of cats in a few years.

Worth repeating at dinner

Trap-neuter-return stabilises community cat colonies humanely.

- ASPCA

Keeping cats indoors protects birds and keeps cats safer.

- American Bird Conservancy
