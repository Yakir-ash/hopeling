Birds

North America lost ~3 billion birds since 1970; windows and cats are key threats.

← The Atlas

The picture

Birds are vital pollinators, seed-spreaders and pest controllers, but North America alone has lost around 3 billion birds since 1970. Simple home fixes - bird-safe glass, native plants, keeping cats in - make a real difference.

The science

Class Aves. Many songbirds migrate thousands of kilometres, navigating by stars and magnetic fields.

Small actions that reach them

Set up a bird feeder & water
Clean feeders and window decals help birds and prevent collisions.
~20 min
Plant one native flower
Native plants feed local pollinators and birds far better than ornamentals.
~30 min
Keep cats indoors or supervised
Free-roaming cats are a leading human-linked cause of bird deaths.
~5 min
Make your windows bird-safe
Up to a billion birds a year die striking glass they can't see.
~20 min
Choose shade-grown or bird-friendly coffee
Shade coffee keeps forest canopy standing - vital habitat for migratory birds.
~5 min
Put up a nest box
Modern buildings lack the cavities swifts, tits and sparrows nest in.
