Penguins

Many penguin species decline as fish stocks and sea ice shift.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernEndangeredExtinct

The picture

Penguins are superbly adapted ocean hunters, but many species are declining as warming seas move their prey and reduce the sea ice some depend on. Marine protected areas help rebuild their food supply.

The science

Family Spheniscidae. Penguins are flightless birds whose wings evolved into powerful flippers.

Worth repeating at dinner

Emperor penguins can dive over 500 m and hold their breath for 20 minutes.

- NOAA

Penguin parents often take turns to incubate eggs and feed at sea.
