Frogs & Amphibians

The most threatened group of vertebrates on Earth; a deadly fungus and habitat loss drive the crisis.

← The Atlas

Least ConcernEndangeredExtinct

The picture

Frogs, toads, salamanders and newts are living indicators of a healthy planet - their thin skin makes them sensitive to pollution and warming. They are also the most endangered vertebrate class, but captive-breeding and wetland restoration are bringing some back from the brink.

The science

Class Amphibia, ~8,700 species. Amphibians breathe partly through their skin, which must stay moist - making them acutely sensitive to pollutants, disease, and drying habitats.

What they face

Chytrid fungus

A skin-attacking fungus has devastated frog populations on nearly every continent.

IUCN

Wetland loss & pollution

Draining ponds and pesticide runoff destroy breeding sites.

Xerces Society

What is already working

Backyard ponds

Small clean ponds give frogs safe places to breed.

Captive breeding

Zoos raise disease-free frogs to re-seed wild populations.

Reasons for hope

Species brought back

Captive-bred corroboree frogs and others are being returned to the wild.

Fungus-resistant frogs

Some populations are evolving resistance to chytrid and recovering.
