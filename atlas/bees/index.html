Bees & Pollinators

Pollinators support ~75% of leading food crops.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernVulnerableExtinct

The picture

Pollinators - bees, butterflies, moths, beetles and more - underpin most of the crops we eat and the wild plants that feed other animals. Habitat loss and pesticides are driving declines, but gardens and pesticide-free spaces help fast.

The science

Superfamily Apoidea and others. Wild native bees often out-pollinate honeybees per flower visit.

In numbers

~75%Leading food crops needing pollinators

Worth repeating at dinner

One in three bites of food we eat depends on pollinators.

- FAO

Most bee species are solitary, not hive-dwellers.

- Xerces Society
