Wetlands

Earth’s most valuable ecosystem per acre - and among the fastest disappearing.

← The Atlas

The picture

Marshes, swamps, bogs and mangroves store carbon, filter water, buffer floods, and shelter an astonishing share of wildlife. We have lost a third of the world’s wetlands since 1970, but restoration is one of the highest-return actions in conservation.

The science

Wetlands cover ~6% of land but support ~40% of all species. Peat wetlands alone store twice as much carbon as all the world’s forests combined.
