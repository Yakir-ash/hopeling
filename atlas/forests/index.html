Forests

Forests host ~80% of land biodiversity; deforestation is slowing in places.

← The Atlas

The picture

Forests store carbon, regulate water and shelter around 80% of land-based species. Agriculture - especially beef, soy and palm oil - drives most deforestation, but certification and rewilding are turning the tide in places.

The science

Tropical forests are the most biodiverse habitats on land, with some single trees hosting hundreds of species.

Small actions that reach them

Switch a product to palm-oil-free
Unsustainable palm oil drives tropical deforestation that destroys orangutan, tiger and elephant habitat.
~5 min
Have one meatless meal
Animal agriculture is a top driver of land use, emissions and farmed-animal suffering.
~30 min
Plant or fund a native tree
Reforestation restores habitat and captures carbon with native species.
~60 min
Choose shade-grown or bird-friendly coffee
Shade coffee keeps forest canopy standing - vital habitat for migratory birds.
~5 min
Buy one thing secondhand instead of new
Making new goods drives mining, logging and emissions that shrink wild habitat.
~15 min
Use a tree-planting search engine
Some search engines fund reforestation with their ad revenue - searching becomes habitat.
~3 min
Buy no new clothes this month
Fashion is a top polluter of waterways and a major carbon source.
~5 min
