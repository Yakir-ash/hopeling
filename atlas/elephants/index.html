Elephants

Keystone species that disperse seeds of over 100 tree species.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernEndangeredExtinct

The picture

Elephants shape entire landscapes - knocking down trees, digging waterholes and spreading seeds - which is why they are called a keystone species. African forest elephants are Critically Endangered; savanna elephants are Endangered.

The science

Genera Loxodonta (Africa) and Elephas (Asia). Elephants show empathy, mourning and self-recognition in mirrors.

In numbers

100+Tree species they help spread

Worth repeating at dinner

Elephants can recognise themselves in a mirror - a rare sign of self-awareness.

They communicate over kilometres using low rumbles humans can barely hear.
