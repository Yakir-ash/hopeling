Lions

Africa has lost most of its lions in a century, but guarded reserves and herder partnerships are turning it around.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernVulnerableExtinct

The picture

Lions once roamed from Africa to India. Today around 20,000 remain in the wild, in a fraction of their old range. The bright spot is that where lions are well protected and where local communities benefit from them living nearby, prides recover. Kenya, Namibia and India's Gir forest all show numbers holding or climbing.

The science

Panthera leo. Lions are the only truly social cats. A pride is built around related females who hunt together and raise cubs communally, and a lion's roar can carry about 8 kilometers.

What they face

Habitat and prey loss

As grasslands become farmland, lions lose both space and the antelope they live on.

IUCN

Conflict with livestock herders

Lions that kill cattle are often killed in return - the single biggest cause of lion deaths.

Panthera

What is already working

Predator-proof bomas

Simple reinforced livestock pens stop night raids, so herders stop losing cattle and lions stop being killed.

Lion Guardians

Former lion hunters are hired and trained to track and protect the same prides - one of conservation's great turnarounds.

Reasons for hope

Herders become guardians

In parts of Kenya, warriors who once speared lions now protect them by name, and killings have dropped dramatically.

Asiatic lions climbing

India's last wild lion population has grown steadily for decades under strict protection.
