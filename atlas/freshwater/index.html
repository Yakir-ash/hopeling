← The Atlas

The picture

Rivers, lakes and wetlands hold a huge share of biodiversity in a tiny share of the world’s water - and freshwater wildlife has declined faster than land or ocean species. Dams, pollution and over-extraction are the main pressures.

The science

Freshwater covers under 1% of Earth’s surface but supports roughly 10% of all known species.

Small actions that reach them

Cut water use today
Freshwater habitats and their species are among the most threatened on Earth.
~5 min
Go pesticide-free in your garden
Pesticides harm pollinators and cause secondary poisoning up the food chain.
~20 min
Refuse one single-use plastic
8M+ tonnes of plastic enter the ocean yearly, harming turtles, seabirds and whales.
~2 min
Catch rainwater for wildlife
Clean water and healthy runoff support amphibians, birds and insects, and ease pressure on rivers.
~45 min
Wash clothes cold and full
Cold, full loads cut energy and shed fewer microplastics into rivers and seas.
~2 min
