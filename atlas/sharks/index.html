Sharks

About a third of sharks and rays face extinction, largely from overfishing.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernVulnerableExtinct

The picture

Sharks are apex predators that keep ocean food webs in balance, yet roughly a third of shark and ray species are threatened. They grow slowly and have few young, so populations recover slowly once overfished.

The science

Class Chondrichthyes (cartilaginous fish). Sharks predate the dinosaurs and even trees, having existed for around 450 million years.

What they face

Overfishing & finning

Demand for fins and slow reproduction drive steep declines.

IUCN

Bycatch

Sharks are caught unintentionally in fisheries targeting other species.

NOAA

What is already working

Shark sanctuaries

Nations are closing waters to commercial shark fishing.

Fin trade bans

Bans on the sale and trade of fins reduce demand.

Reasons for hope

Palau’s sanctuary

Palau protected its entire exclusive economic zone from commercial shark fishing.
