Bats

Vital night pollinators and pest-eaters; white-nose syndrome and habitat loss are the big threats.

← The Atlas
Least ConcernVulnerableExtinct

The picture

Bats are the only truly flying mammals and quiet heroes of the night - pollinating plants, spreading seeds, and eating vast numbers of insect pests. A fungal disease and roost loss have hit many species hard, but bat boxes and cave protection are helping.

The science

Order Chiroptera, ~1,400 species - a fifth of all mammals. Most insect-eating bats navigate by echolocation, emitting calls and reading the echoes to hunt in total darkness.

What they face

White-nose syndrome

A cold-loving fungus wakes hibernating bats, burning the fat they need to survive winter.

USGS

Roost loss

Sealed caves, felled old trees, and disturbed attics remove safe places to rest.

IUCN

What is already working

Bat boxes

Putting up roost boxes gives displaced bats a safe home.

Cave gates

Bat-friendly gates keep people out while letting bats fly free.

Reasons for hope

Cave protection works

Gating key hibernation caves has stabilized some bat colonies.

Farmers value bats

Bats save farmers billions a year in natural pest control, driving new protections.
