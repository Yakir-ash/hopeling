# Hopeling.app - The Complete Website

## The one decision everything else follows from

You are one person. Apple, Notion, and Patagonia have content teams feeding their websites daily. If we copy their sitemaps, we build a beautiful ghost town: a Blog with three posts, a Community page with no community, a Careers page for a company that does not exist. Nothing kills trust faster than a website that is pretending.

So the founding rule of hopeling.app is:

**Do not build a website you have to feed. Build a website that feeds itself.**

Hopeling already owns something almost no startup has: a living content engine. The news bot updates content.json every Monday and Thursday. It archives every aged-out story into wins[]. The app already carries 26 worlds, hundreds of species, hundreds of facts with sources, courses, guardians, and a real global counter. That is not app data. That is a website that has been writing itself for months.

The website is therefore not a brochure ABOUT the app. It is the app's knowledge, opened to the whole internet, readable without downloading anything. The app becomes the ritual; the website becomes the library. Same soul, same content contract, two doors.

And one positioning sentence that beats every competitor page on the internet:

**Wikipedia tells you the vaquita is dying. Hopeling tells you that, then hands you one thing to do before dinner.** Every encyclopedia page on this site ends in agency. No other nature site does this.

## The sitemap

### Tier 1 - The living pages (why people return)

**/ - Home.** Already built. The door: living sky, the rain, the story, the pulse. It stays the emotional overture; everything below becomes the rooms behind it. Nav appears at top.

**/today - The Daily Page.** Today's fact and today's action, computed with the same dailyIndex hash as the app, so the web and the app always agree on what today holds. Changes at midnight. A "share as plate" poster button. This is the page people bookmark, set as a browser homepage, open with coffee. Why it exists: it turns the website itself into a daily habit, app or no app. Return trigger: it is different every single day, forever, with zero work from you.

**/wins - While The World Worried.** The wins[] archive rendered as an endless scroll of only good news: dated, sourced, filterable by animal and by year. Tagline on the page: "The only news feed that gets better the longer you scroll." Why it exists: nobody on the internet keeps a permanent, sourced archive of wildlife good news. In five years this is the largest one that exists, and it grew two entries at a time while you slept. Who it is for: the doomscroller at 1am, the journalist looking for a counter-narrative, the teacher who needs one hopeful thing for Monday. This page IS the blog: bot-fed, never stale, never a graveyard.

**/atlas - The Atlas.** The 26 worlds as an explorable index, each with its own atmosphere wash. Each world page (/atlas/oceans, /atlas/forests...) carries its overview, science, threats, hope, its species, and its actions. Beneath the worlds: a page per species (/species/vaquita, /species/amur-leopard...), generated from content.json plus the Wikipedia and GBIF data the app already fetches. Photo full-bleed, the IUCN bar drawing itself, population, one fact, and always, at the bottom: "One thing you can do for them today." Why it exists: this is the SEO engine and the curiosity engine in one body. Hundreds of pages from data you already maintain.

**/guardians - The Rarest.** One page per guardian species, written like a portrait, not a report: the Kakapo where every bird has a name, the ten remaining vaquitas. Live-updated counts when sources allow. The pledge works on the web too: press and hold, get a beautiful certificate image to keep or share, and a gentle "your pledge will be waiting in the app." Why it exists: it is the single most emotionally shareable content Hopeling has, and pledge certificates are free advertising drawn in gold.

**/rain - The Rain.** A full-screen, nearly empty page: the sky at your real hour, the live counter, and a drop of rain for actions as they accumulate, with a soft sound if you allow it. No nav, no text beyond one line. Why it exists: it is a window, not a page. People leave it open on a second monitor, in classrooms, on a TV. No environmental site has an ambient page. Cost: one evening. Memorability: permanent.

### Tier 2 - The library (the 5-to-10-year authority)

**/learn - Field Guides.** The app's courses rendered as beautifully typeset, fully readable chapters on the open web, serif type, no login, no teaser-paywall. Quizzes work in the page. Why open: locked content earns nothing; open content earns links, teachers, and trust, and every chapter ends with "carry this in your pocket."

**/facts - The Fact Vault.** Every fact in the corpus gets a permanent URL (/facts/sharks-before-trees) rendered as a museum plate: photo, serif, source. One button: download as poster. Why it exists: facts are Hopeling's most shareable atom, and each plate URL is a tiny SEO net for "amazing facts about X" searches. The daily plate on /today links here.

**/kids - Little Helpers.** The cartoon film, the simplest facts (simpleText already exists in the schema), and printable PDFs: coloring pages, a week-of-small-deeds checklist, "my species" mini-posters. Big type, no reading walls, nothing to buy. Why it exists: parents search "nature activities for kids" constantly; printables are the currency of that world and they get pinned to refrigerators, which is the best ad placement on Earth.

**/classroom - For Teachers.** Not a separate product, a generous shelf: a weekly class pack auto-built from the current fact, action, and win (printable, one page); an "Adopt a Species" class project guide using the Atlas; the films with discussion questions. Why it exists: one teacher equals thirty children a year, every year. Schools are the compounding channel, and this page costs almost nothing because it re-dresses existing content.

### Tier 3 - The trust pages (short, honest, done)

**/about - The Honest Page.** No corporate mission statement. The true story in first person: one person who got tired of feeling helpless about the news and built a small app about hope, with the films embedded. Ends with the mission line and a photo of the grove, not a headshot wall. Why it exists: for a one-person project, honesty is not a limitation, it is the brand moat. Nobody can out-sincere you.

**/press - Press Kit.** Both films downloadable, the icon, screenshots light and dark, the one-paragraph story, key facts (free, no ads, no tracking, works offline, real counter), contact. Why it exists: when a journalist gives you eight minutes, this page is the difference between coverage and silence.

**/privacy** - exists. Stays plain and proud; it is one of the best marketing pages you have.

**Contact** - the mailto in the footer. It does not need a page.

### Pages deliberately refused

- **Careers, Team, Investors:** there is no company. Pretending would poison the About page.
- **Blog / Journal:** a blog you cannot feed weekly is a countdown to embarrassment. /wins is the blog and the bot feeds it.
- **Community / Forum:** moderation is a full-time job; the app's Circles ARE the community, kept small and safe on purpose.
- **Carbon Footprint Calculator:** off-mission. Hopeling is wildlife and hope, not guilt accounting. A hundred sites do calculators; none do the Fact Vault.
- **Interactive globe / live migration map:** breathtaking, and a data-licensing and engineering swamp. Parked for the year the site earns it; the Atlas delivers geography-as-wonder at one percent of the cost.
- **Organizations / Partners:** appears the day a real partnership exists, not before. Until then orgs live inside each world page where they already do.

## Navigation and footer

**Top nav (5 items, never more):** Today · Atlas · Wins · Learn · Kids, plus one button: "Open the app". Wordmark left (lowercase hopeling, gold tittle). On scroll the nav glasses over the living sky. Mobile: same five in a sheet.

**Footer - the forest floor.** Dark nightsoil band on every page: the wordmark, the line "small actions, real hope", the sitemap in one row, Privacy, Press, Contact, and the small honest sentence: "Made by one person and a growing forest of helpers." Nothing else.

**Search.** One box, opened with / anywhere: searches species, worlds, facts, and wins from a small prebuilt index (client-side, no server, instant). Results grouped: "In the Atlas / In the Vault / In the Wins." For a content site this size, search is a feature most visitors will remember.

## User journeys

**The doomscroller (1am, phone).** Lands on /wins from a shared link. Scrolls. Feels the knot loosen. Bookmarks it. Two visits later notices "carry the hope in your pocket" and installs. Design requirement: /wins must load in under a second and scroll forever without a single popup.

**The kid with homework (school laptop).** Googles "how many amur leopards are left", lands on /species/amur-leopard, gets the number, the photo, the drawn IUCN bar, and cites the sources block. Copies the "one thing you can do" into the conclusion of the essay. Design requirement: species pages answer the question in the first screenful, with a real citations section.

**The teacher (Sunday evening).** Searches "wildlife activities for class", finds /classroom, prints the weekly pack in one click. Returns weekly because the pack rebuilds itself twice a week. Design requirement: every printable is a clean, ink-friendly PDF, zero signup.

**The parent (weekend).** Finds /kids via Pinterest or a friend, watches Little Helpers with the child, prints a coloring page, and does one small deed together. The fridge poster does the retention. Design requirement: /kids is fully usable by a seven-year-old alone.

Every journey ends at content, not at a signup wall. The app link is always present, never demanding. That restraint is why people send the site to friends.

## Design system - Dawnlight, sitewide

The website inherits the app's design language (DESIGN.md) so the whole ecosystem is one organism:

- **Canvas:** Paper #FAF8F2 by day; the living sky (dawn/day/dusk/night, already built on Home) crowns every page's header at the visitor's real hour. The site literally never looks the same twice.
- **Type:** serif (Georgia stack, later Fraunces) for wonder - facts, species names, story; system sans for UI. Small-caps letterspaced labels.
- **Color:** Ink, Fern, Hopelight mint, Deepwater; Seedlight gold RESERVED for pledges and precious moments only.
- **The one-living-thing rule:** every page has exactly one living element - rain on /wins headers, the drawing IUCN bar on species pages, the growing tree on Home, the counter on /rain. Never two. Calm is the luxury.
- **Motion:** reveal-on-scroll, 250ms, ease-out, and prefers-reduced-motion kills all of it.
- **Photography:** full-bleed, dignified, sourced (Wikipedia/Wikimedia pipeline the app already uses). No stock-photo smiling people holding globes. Ever.
- **Mobile:** single column, same soul; on mobile the "Open the app" button becomes the primary action because the phone IS the app's home.

## SEO - the ten-year plan

1. **Static HTML for everything.** A build script (scripts/build-site.mjs) generates every world, species, fact, and win page as real HTML from content.json at deploy time. The same GitHub Action that runs the news bot rebuilds the site Mon+Thu. The site grows twice a week, automatically, forever. No JavaScript-rendered content, so every page is fully crawlable on day one.
2. **Question-shaped pages.** Species pages target the questions people actually type: "how many vaquitas are left", "is the kakapo extinct", "what do pangolins eat". H1s and meta descriptions written as answers.
3. **The wins moat.** "Good news about tigers" is a search with real volume and almost no supply. Each win page is dated, sourced, permanent. Archives compound; news sites bury.
4. **Structured data everywhere:** Article on wins, Taxon/Species markup on species pages, FAQ markup on question blocks, Organization on the root.
5. **The internal mesh:** species link to their world, their guardian, their wins, and their action; every plate links to its species. Crawlers and children follow the same paths.
6. **Printables and plates earn backlinks** from teacher blogs and Pinterest - the two most durable link ecosystems for this niche.
7. **Never chase:** no keyword-stuffed listicles, no "10 best eco apps" self-ranking, no AI-sludge posts. Authority in this niche will be won by the only site whose every fact has a source and whose every page ends in agency.

## How it gets built (phases, honest sizing)

**Phase 1 - The engine (one session).** scripts/build-site.mjs + a shared site.css: generates /today, /wins, and all /species pages from content.json. The site goes from 1 page to 300+ pages in one commit, and the bot keeps it growing.

**Phase 2 - The Atlas and the Guides (one to two sessions).** World pages, /learn chapters, /facts plates, client-side search index.

**Phase 3 - The people pages (one session).** /kids with printables, /classroom weekly pack generator, /about, /press.

**Phase 4 - The wonders (when earned).** /rain, web pledges with certificates, seasonal events (a Migration Week, a Night of the Owls), and, in the far seat, the globe.

**Tech stance:** no framework, no build tool beyond one Node script, no CMS, no server. GitHub Pages serves it all. content.json remains the single source of truth (Contract 2 in FLUTTER-CONTRACTS.md), which means the future Flutter app, the PWA, and the website all drink from the same well and can never disagree.

## The measure of success

Not signups. Not bounce rate. The measure is the sentence you asked for: someone arrives to check out an app and leaves forty minutes later having read about the kakapo, scrolled a year of good news, printed a coloring page, and felt lighter. Some of them will install the app. All of them will remember where the website lives - because on the whole internet, this is the door where the news only gets better.
